Bio
Currently I am working as Research Scientist in Parallel Computing Lab (PCL), part of Intel Lab, focusing on architecture for newer and emerging applications like Deep Learning. Before that I completed my PhD in Electrical and Computer Engineering at University of Maryland, College Park in Spring 2014. My research interest lies in the broader field of computer architecture with a focus on memory systems and Deep Learning. Particularly, my PhD research work was focused around the designing scalable and energy-efficient DRAM based systems. My PhD advisor is Prof. Bruce Jacob, we collaborate with Zeshan Chishti and Shih-Lien Lu from Intel Labs.
Before joining UMD, I worked in industry for five years, playing key roles in design and verification of complex ASIC chips. I received B.Tech. in Electronics and Communication Engineering from Indian Institute of Technology(IIT), Guwahati (2005).

Research
  • Scalable DRAM Refresh
    • Comprehensive evaluation and survey of DRAM refresh mechanisms, trade-offs and penalties. We clarify prevalent confusions with refresh options and timings available in JEDEC specified DDR devices.
    • Simulated DRAM devices with different configurations to demonstrate increasing refresh penalties. This study is under review in Transactions on Computers.
    • Proposed simple modification in DRAM device to enable refreshes reduction with optimized auto-refresh commands rather than in-efficient row-level refresh commands. Paper under review in a conference.
    • Designing an automatic mechanism to dynamically schedule per-bank, all-bank or finer-granularity refresh commands to achieve better performance and energy.
  • Energy Efficient Memory Systems
    • Proposed novel techniques to simultaneously minimize two important types of DRAM energy components: background and refresh.
    • Our novel schemes called "Coordinated Refresh" schedule refresh operations and power down modes in an energy efficient fashion.
    • Devised a new History based Memory Activity Predictor (HMAP) which is simple to implement yet more accurate than prior predictors.
    • This work is accepted for presentation in ISLPED'13 .
  • High Capacity Non-Volatile Memory (NVM)
    • Contributed in the design of parametrized simulation infrastructure to study NVMs, with a focus on utilizing commodity NAND Flash as main memory candidate.
    • Explored emerging NVM technologies in order to find their appropriate position in the memory hierarchy using range of target applications.
    • This project is funded by Intel Corporation and initial infrastructure published in ITJ,2013.
  • Accurate Memory Simulations
    • Designed set of techniques, when applied in a full-system simulator give reliable, accurate and less variable results.
    • Our techniques implemented on MARSSx86 integrated with DRAMSim2 for case study to show reduce variability in simulations.
    • Related Tech Report.
